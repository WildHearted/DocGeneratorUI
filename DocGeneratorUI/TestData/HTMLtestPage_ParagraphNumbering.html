Paragraph Numbering

This test case validates the numbering of paragraphs.

There are a few types of paragraphs:

  1. Normal Paragraphs
  2. Headings
  3. Organised Lists
  4. Unorganised Lists

Normal Paragraphs

Just to make sure we are all on the same page, here is the definition of a paragraph: "A distinct division of written or printed matter that begins on a new, usually indented line, consists of one or more sentences, and typically deals with a single thought or topic or quotes one speaker's continuous words.".

The "section" of text is a separate from the previous section and demarckated as separate "paragraph".

Headings

A heading is in fact also paragraph. However, the purpose of a heading is to group and title a set of paragraphs into topics or themes.

Organised Lists

Organised Lists are also paragraphs, but these paragraphs are usually preceded by a numeric number. It is also known as Numbered Lists. The purpose of organised lists or numbered lists, is to indicate a specific order of paragraphs. If the order of the paragraphs are not important, then it is more appropriate to use an Unorganised List or a Bullet List that has the same purpose but for which the order if of not relevant.

Under certain conditions it may be necessary to cascade a numbered list into multiple levels to indicate a hierarchy.

  1. This is the 1st entry for the level 1 numbered paragraph.
  2. This is the 2nd entry for the level 1 numbered paragraph.
    1. This is the 1st entry for the level 2 paragraph.

    2. This is the 2nd entry for the level 2 paragraph.

      1. This is the 1st entry for the level 3 paragraph.

      2. This is the 2nd entry for the level 3 paragraph.

    3. This is the 3nd entry for the level 2 paragraph.
  3. This is the 3rd entry for the level 1 paragraph.

More normal paragraphs can occur here and then we need to begin with a new hierarchical numbered list. This numbered list continues where the previous list ended. It doesn't restart the numbering, it just continue the numbering.

  1. This is the 4th entry for the level 1 numbered paragraph.
  2. This is the 5th entry for the level 1 numbered paragraph.
    1. This is the 1st entry for the level 2 paragraph.

    2. This is the 2nd entry for the level 2 paragraph.

      1. This is the 1st entry for the level 3 paragaph.

      2. This is the 2nd entry for the level 3 paragraph.

    3. This is the 3nd entry for the level 2 paragraph.
  3. This is the 6th entry for the level 1 paragraph.

Then a point is reached where a new hierarchy of a numbered list begin. The next numbered list needs start numbering at one and NOT continue as was the case in the previous example.

  1. This is the 1st entry for the level 1 numbered paragraph.
  2. This is the 2nd entry for the level 1 numbered paragraph.
    1. This is the 1st entry for the level 2 paragraph.

    2. This is the 2nd endtry for the level 2 paragraph.

      1. This is the 1st entry for the level 3 paragraph.

      2. This is the 2nd entry for the level 3 paragraph.

    3. This is the 3nd entry for the level 2 paragraph.
  3. This is the 3rd entry for the level 1 paragraph.

This concludes paragraph numbering testing.
